EduChat Writeup

As I note, I did most of my development for this assignment in the format of a mobile-style screen, as that is what I predict to be the most common use case for my app. This format is also what I tested in my paper and figma prototypes. Due to this, although my design is universal, I suggest a mobile format (through Inspect) for a better user experience.

The final design I plan to hand in for this assignment is similar to my figma mock-up, with the tentative file/link pinning feature. My design can be subdivided into 2/3 main points of development: group chats, classes, and the file/link pinning feature.

For this assignment, as discussed with instructors in studio, I concluded that a good halfway point would be implementing the entire group chats functionality. This went beyond my wireframes, as I had to deal with group chat editing, participant toggling, etc. Based on the format of Graffiti there have been a few changes. Now, group chats have an admin (the creator of the chat), and members can only be added/removed by them. If a member wishes to leave the group, they must submit a request, which the admin automatically approves once they are online. Moreover, only an admin can edit the group chat’s name. I believe this portion of my design takes up half, if not more, of the coding/work that I will have to do.

The classes I hope to implement in my final iteration simply consist of creating static Graffiti types representing classes (which participants can join or leave without requesting - I plan to do this by using Join types attached to a class id). Once these have been implemented, it is simply a matter of assigning their ids to their respective groupchats’ contexts, and limiting group chat participants to those who have joined the respective class.

I leave the file/link pinning feature as a tentative add-on. Although I think it is very useful, I recognize that the main parts of my app consist on the class-group chat hierarchy, and I want to prioritize the usability of these before adding my third element.
